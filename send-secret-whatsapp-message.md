## Understanding Text Steganography

Steganography is the practice of concealing a file, message, image, or video within another file or message. Our Invisible Text Generator applies this cryptographic concept to standard text inputs.

Instead of encrypting your message into a garbled string of random characters (which immediately draws suspicion), this module encodes your sensitive payload into **Zero-Width Characters (ZWC)**. These are valid Unicode characters that computers can read, but are completely invisible to the human eye.

### How the Payload is Concealed
When you type a secret message, the algorithm converts it into binary data. It then maps this binary sequence into a combination of zero-width spaces, non-joiners, and joiners. Finally, it wraps this invisible payload around a "decoy" message (e.g., "Hello, how are you?").

To anyone looking at your phone, they only see the decoy message. However, the true payload remains hidden in plain sight, stored entirely within the blank spaces of the text.
